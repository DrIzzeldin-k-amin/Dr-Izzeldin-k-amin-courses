General Objectives of Course

The course explores the functions of an operating system. It concentrates on the structure, how to use an operating system within programming, and gives examples of operating systems in use.

General Objectives:

  • Internal structure of an operating system.
  • Processes and threads and how to create them and manage their execution.
  • Concurrency and synchronization.
  • Memory and virtual memory management.
  • Different types of mass storage devices.
  • I/O systems.
  • File systems structures and their uses.
  • Virtual machines.
  • Protection and security.
Intended Learning Outcomes (ILOs)
  • Knowledge and Understanding: Understand the main functions of an operating system and how to use them.
  • Intellectual Skill: Learn the mechanisms of using main operating system functions and explore new developments in the field.
  • Professional and Practical Skills: Utilize the main functions of an operating system and understand their purposes.
  • General and Transferable Skills: Master the steps of booting an operating system and determine how to add new features or drivers to the system.
Teaching and Learning Methods
  1. Recorded Lectures.
  2. Online Quizzes.
  3. Online Problem Solving and Discussion.
  4. Self-Learning through Guided Lab-Experiments.
  5. Problem Sheets to be solved and submitted.
Evaluation Methods
  • Final Exam: 60% (Week 14)
  • Midterm Exams: 25%
  • Lab Projects (Practical): 15%
Instructions for Students
  1. Download lectures on-time and study them carefully.
  2. Read corresponding chapters from the prescribed textbook.
  3. Perform lab experiments or collaborate with classmates.
  4. Complete quizzes and submit assignments on time.
  5. Review past exam papers when available.
