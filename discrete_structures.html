General Objectives of Course

This course introduces students to ideas and techniques from discrete mathematical structures widely used in computer science and IT. It teaches techniques for logical and mathematical thinking and applies them to solve problems. Topics covered include logic, proof, sets, functions, sequences, mathematical induction, recursion, and group theory for cryptography.

General Objectives:

  • Appreciate Boolean algebra, logic, and set theory.
  • Understand logical arguments and constructs, as well as sets and functions.
  • Use mathematical reasoning techniques including induction and recursion.
  • Communicate solutions to technical problems effectively.
  • Demonstrate traversal methods for trees and graphs.
  • Model problems in computer science using graphs and trees.
Intended Learning Outcomes (ILOs)
  • Knowledge and Understanding: Think mathematically to solve real problems in computer applications.
  • Intellectual Skills: Develop logical thinking and use methods from mathematical logic to solve real-life problems in innovative ways.
  • Professional and Practical Skills: Develop models for real-life cases, formulate problems, and find suitable solutions.
  • General and Transferable Skills: Express logical solutions to real-life problems and solve them using discrete structures (logic, combinatorics, graphs, etc.).
Teaching and Learning Methods
  1. Recorded Lectures.
  2. Online Quizzes.
  3. Online Problem Solving and Discussion.
  4. Self-Learning through System Programming Lab Experiments.
  5. Problem Sheets to be solved and submitted.
Evaluation Methods
  • Final Exam: 60% (Week 14)
  • Midterm Exam: 20% (Week 7)
  • Tutorials: 20% (Weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12)
Instructions for Students
  1. Download lectures on time and study them carefully.
  2. Read the corresponding chapters from the prescribed textbook.
  3. Perform lab experiments and collaborate with classmates.
  4. Complete quizzes and submit assignments on time.
  5. Review past exam papers when available.
